## How I Work and Think

I'm someone who learns by building. When I encounter a new concept, my instinct is to find the smallest project that forces me to actually use it. I've been deliberate about breaking the habit of leaning on AI tools as a first-response debugging mechanism — I want to develop genuine intuition for systems, not just the ability to prompt my way to an answer.

I think a lot about product decisions, not just technical ones. When I'm building something I'm always asking: why does this exist, who is it for, and what does it feel like to use it? That's part of why I gravitate toward consumertech and social media — the feedback loop between what you build and how people actually experience it is tight and honest.

I'm also comfortable with ambiguity. Most of my best work has started from a messy problem with no clear solution, and I've had to figure out the architecture, the product logic, and the execution at the same time.
